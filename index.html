Campus Research: Renewable Energy Law LLM Analysis & Database Project

Technologies: LangChain, LiteLLM, RAG, ChromaDB, MySQL

In a research project with Dr. Ji, a UF Food and Resource Economist, I developed a model using LangChain and ChromaDB to compare different LLMs' analysis of renewable energy laws documents with up to 80% accuracy. The pipeline used UF hosted LLM's like ChatGPT and Mistral and combined with publicly accessible LlaMa and Anthropic LLM's.

Additionally, I created a comprehensive academic database under Dr. Butler of FICS (Florida Institute of Cybersecurity) to store and retrieve conference papers that is bigger than Google Scholar's resources for CS papers. I aggregated all of the notable conference papers from ACSAC, IEEE, AsiaCCS, etc. and made them queryable by conference, year, author, and also created functionality for authors and their co-authors, as well as search for papers by keywords in the paper.

These projects were a great way to get experience with real-world applications of AI and databases, and I was able to work with some of the best professors to get a better understanding of the research process.
